Public proof record 07 / launchpad

A fair start, precisely

A fair launch is a theorem.

One price for a round. The full supply disclosed. A trading pool without a hidden withdrawal handle.

Present scope: the market parts are proven; the launchpad product does not exist to launch on today.

01

How a launch is rigged

Three doors the operator controls.

01

Bots take the first slice

Fast buyers capture the first cheap tokens and sell them to slower people seconds later.

02

Supply hides in fresh wallets

A creator or friend holds an undisclosed stash, then sells it into the crowd.

03

The liquidity leaves

Someone with a withdrawal handle drains the pool and leaves everyone else unable to sell.

02

The proven boundary

Not discouraged. Unbuildable.

The first three records below are machine-checked theorems in the market machinery. The fourth is a design, not a theorem or finished product.

proven

No first slice to snipe

Everyone in a round buys at one price, set by the sealed-batch market. There is no first order for a bot to race toward.

proven

No hidden supply door

The full supply is disclosed in advance. New supply can only enter through a recorded, authorized turn.

proven

No liquidity pull

The pool remains solvent under every sequence of trades, and nobody holds a secret withdrawal handle.

designed

A bond for a broken promise

A creator who promises to hold, then sells early, loses a launch deposit to the holders—not to the platform.

03

Where proof stops

A fair launch is not a good investment.

A fair start means an honest distribution, disclosed supply, and no rigged wheel. It cannot make the project ship, make the idea good, or keep the price from going to zero.

A bad token stays bad.

A fairly launched token can still be worthless. Mathematics does not decide whether an idea deserves demand.

Some behavior sits outside the proof.

One person can use many wallets. Fake trading volume can be made for attention. Those cases require detection and judgment.
