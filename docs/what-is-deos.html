What is deos?

· a two-minute read ·

deos is a verified object-capability computer you can't be lied to about — an agentic desktop where every action is a proof, not a promise.

The one idea

A normal computer asks you to trust it: trust that the app did what it said, that the log wasn't edited, that the balance is real, that the file on disk is the file you saved. deos removes the trust. Underneath the desktop is dregg — a formally verified, capability-secure substrate where the unit of everything is a cell (a card, a document, an agent, a room, a service, you) and the only way anything changes is a turn: one authorized step, gated before it commits, chained by a receipt that binds the whole new state.

Two consequences make deos different from every desktop you've used:

You can only do what you hold a capability for — and you hold a capability exactly when you can produce its witness. An affordance (a button, a menu item) is just a cap-gated turn template. Sharing is handing over an attenuated capability, never a copy of your authority. Nothing amplifies; re-sharing only ever narrows.

Nobody has to take the history on faith. Every turn carries a succinct proof that it was a legal transition, and the whole chain folds into one constant-size root. So:

A light client holding one root knows every transition in the whole history was authorized, conservative, fresh, and correctly committed — re-executing nothing. That sentence is the system.

The desktop is a fold over the receipt chain. “The substrate cannot lie” is not a slogan here — it is a column you can read, a rail you can scrub, and a refusal that lands on the glass in amber.
