The MULTIPLAYER MEMBRANE is the fork-and-stitch primitive — the killer move where a *message* is a cap-bounded world-fork you drive and stitch back. It is captured by the shared_fork suite (21 tests on the real embedded executor) — cd starbridge-v2 && cargo test --features native-full shared_fork.

What it shows: ONE minted MembraneFrustum (a cap-bounded World::fork cull — the screenshot-of-the-moment) is carried over the postcard wire shape (the MembraneEnvelope.snapshot bytes), then rehydrated into TWO independent real Worlds under TWO distinct principals — both frustum_root-matched, which is the anti-substitution check (you cannot swap in a different world behind the frustum). Each principal drives a real verified commit_turn (one overlapping conflict + one disjoint private edit). The two are stitched via Stitch::settle: the disjoint case is a clean pushout-merge; the overlapping divergence resolves by the linear Dead-wins join TRANSPARENTLY (not a silent last-writer-wins); an over-authorized confer is REFUSED as a lossy drop (SettleOutcome::Refused). Σδ=0 and authority stay sound throughout.

What it demonstrates: distributed time-travel with consent — branch a bounded view of the world, let two parties evolve it independently, and merge with conflicts surfaced as first-class objects rather than overwritten, while conservation and the attenuation lattice hold across the whole fork→drive→stitch arc.
