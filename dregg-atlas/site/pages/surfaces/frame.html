THE FRAME · five modes surface

The coherent cockpit shell — ONE persistent chrome, not 20 doors in a dark hallway. A TOP BAR (your identity cell + cap-badge · the live ledger clock · the ⌘K palette · the ⌘J dock toggle), a LEFT RAIL of the FIVE MODES (Inhabit · Author · Dev · Inspect · Operate — one click switches the whole pane's intent), a mode SUB-NAV of that mode's surfaces, and a collapsible DEV DOCK. The 30 surfaces are re-homed under the five modes (a total partition — none deleted); the rail teaches the shape once. See `frame.rs` · `docs/deos/COCKPIT-UX.md`.

The FRAME is the persistent chrome every surface renders inside — the coherence that replaces a flat palette of ~30 peer surfaces with one shape you learn once (cockpit/frame.rs, docs/deos/COCKPIT-UX.md). The cockpit's render (cockpit/render.rs) lays it out top-to-bottom: a TOP BAR, then a row of the LEFT RAIL beside the MAIN PANE (its own mode SUB-NAV above the active surface), and a collapsible DEV DOCK at the bottom.

What it shows: the top bar (Cockpit::top_bar) names who you are — the operator identity cell, a you · <short> pill, and a 🔑 N caps cap-badge counting the capabilities in its c-list — beside the live ledger clock (⛓ h{height} and the ◷ latest-receipt hash, the world's pulse read off the embedded executor), with the ⌘K · find anything palette summon and the ⌘J · dock toggle on the right. The left rail (Cockpit::mode_rail) is the FIVE MODES — Inhabit (🏡 *your living world*), Author (✎ *make things*), Dev (⌨ *the IDE*), Inspect (🔍 *understand*), Operate (⚙ *the machinery*) — each a glyph + label + blurb; the active mode is highlighted. The sub-nav (Cockpit::mode_subnav) chips the surfaces re-homed under the active mode. The dev dock (Cockpit::dev_dock, ⌘J) surfaces the IDE surfaces as quick-jump chips in any mode.

How you use it: click a rail mode to switch the whole pane's intent (it opens that mode's primary surface via set_mode → set_tab); click a sub-nav chip to switch surfaces within the mode; ⌘K to find any command; ⌘J to raise the dev strip. The re-framing is a total partition: every one of the 30 Tabs is re-homed under exactly one mode (Tab::mode is the inverse of CockpitMode::surfaces; the every_tab_has_a_mode + the_twenty_surfaces_partition_across_five_modes tests guard it), and no surface is deleted — the active mode is *derived* from the active surface's mode, so a palette Go<Surface> jump also moves the rail. The v1 frame is gpui (coherence now); the surfaces becoming deos-js cards is the reflective turn that makes the frame yours to reshape from within.
