      What is actually real here

      • The AI narrates freely, but every move is resolved by resolve_action against the world's own rules. A locked exit, an absent item, or an unbeaten Warden is refused deterministically — no prose talks past it.
      • A legal move lands as one verified turn on the receipt rail (narrate_game_move): authentic (fixture leg), well-formed, injection-free, and prev-linked on-chain, binding its typed GameAction ‖ room. A refused move lands no receipt (the anti-ghost tooth).
      • A Take is a cap-permitted grant on the dungeon's own item whitelist (DmCaps::narrator) — the AI cannot mint an item the world never placed.
      • The truth is the ledger, not the story. Restart, and the same gates bite from the shore.

      Honest scope: the narration is a real local model (gemma2:2b) when reachable, else a deterministic scripted narrator (shown honestly above). The attestation's "authentic" leg is an in-tree fixture. What is load-bearing is the world resolution, the capability gate, and the receipt hash-chain.
