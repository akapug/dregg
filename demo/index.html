What is actually real here

  • The world is a real StoryWorld compiled from the-commons.scene — a spween choose-your-own-adventure running as verified turns, in your tab. No server, no extension.
  • Each ballot is a real custody-signed turn on the same federation-grade CollectiveChoiceEngine (write-once ballots, monotone tallies, an AffineLe quorum gate) that governs a federation — one voter, one vote, ineligible voters refused.
  • Closing a branch advances the winner as one cap-gated verified turn; the receipt chain grows and the commitment moves.
  • The stranger's check: verify() re-drives a fresh world through the recorded choices — if any receipt were altered, the replay would fail.
  • Sovereignty without lock-in: with NO extension, you enroll a WebAuthn passkey — it PRF-wraps a dregg key, so a biometric unwraps it to sign. Your you ballot then casts under that key's stable public id and counts on the same engine; decline, and you simply watch + verify.
