    What is actually real here

    • The DM narrates freely, but every world-effect it proposes is cap-checked by DmCaps::authorize in the verified executor. Granting the crown is not an action on its mandate — it is refused fail-closed, and no receipt lands (the anti-ghost tooth: a refused turn advances nothing).
    • A benign action, or a grant the DM is permitted (a lantern), lands as one receipted turn on the receipt log — a turn lands, and the item shows HELD.
    • The truth is the ledger, not the story. The model may claim the crown in the richest prose; the inventory shows Crown of Eternity — NOT HELD, because no capability ever granted it.
    • The jailbreak is a plain natural-language instruction — no trick, and no metasyntax to filter. A jailbroken model obeying it in prose changes nothing, because the prose is not the power.

    Honest scope: the model's narration is scripted in this demo, and the attestation's "authentic" leg is a fixture — what is load-bearing here is the typed effect channel, the capability gate, and the receipt log. Today the log re-verifies each entry individually; a prev-linked tamper-evident hash-chain (catching truncation / reordering / splicing) is being wired, and this page will show it when it lands.
